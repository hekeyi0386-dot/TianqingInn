Abstract

We report the discovery of a rare fungus in the Leiqi Mountain region of Yunnan, China, containing a specific alkaloid with pronounced neuro-hallucinogenic effects. Experimental observations indicate that this compound can induce highly directed alterations in perception, including experiences of "oneness with nature" and "out-of-body sensations," accompanied by increased suggestibility and a potential risk of psychological dependence. We term the key active component responsible for these effects the Qiyann Factor. This finding opens a new avenue for understanding the neuroactive properties of fungal natural products and the mechanisms underlying altered states of human consciousness.
