Interview Transcript

Reporter:
Mr. Yang, many people say Tianqing Homestay has a special charm that makes people not want to leave once they come. Can you share the story of founding the homestay?
Yang Liqiang:
(Smiles warmly) To be honest, before starting this homestay, my life was quite a failure. I ran several small restaurants in the city, tried Sichuan cuisine, hot pot, but they all closed down. During that time, I was deeply in debt and felt hopeless. At my lowest point, I even thought about suicide.
Reporter:
What helped you pull through that low point?
Yang Liqiang:
It was an old friend of mine. He came to me and said, "Liqiang, go back to the mountains. Your roots are there." He was the one who helped me start this "Tianqing Homestay." (Eyes filled with gratitude) You could say, without him, I wouldn't be where I am today.
Reporter:
It sounds like this old friend was your benefactor. How did it feel returning to Jiasa Town?
Yang Liqiang:
(Voice becomes firm and reverent) Jiasa Town is truly my lucky place. The environment here nurtures people, and the mist from Leqi Mountain seems to wash away worries. It was here that I rediscovered peace and a direction in life.
